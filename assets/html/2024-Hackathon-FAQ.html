Hackathon FAQ

How do I form a team?
You can either form a team before the Hackathon and register together, or if you don’t have a team, register individually. During a live Zoom meeting on October 4, 2024, participants needing team members will have the opportunity to post about themselves and connect with others to form a team.
What if I don’t have a team? What can I do?
If you don't have a team, you can still register. Organizers will review registration information and create teams for participants who don't have team members, taking into consideration their skill sets (and age, if applicable). On October 4 at 2 pm, organizers will post a Google Sheet listing all teams formed from individual participants and teams with additional members. Organizers will encourage these teams to communicate with each other and finalize their team compositions by October 9 if any adjustments are needed.
How old do I need to be to participate in the Hackathon?
You must be at least 13 years old to participate. If you are under 18, a parent or guardian must sign a consent form for your participation.
How old do I need to be to win prizes in the Hackathon?
The same age requirement applies: you must be at least 13 years old, and those under 18 need to submit a Parent Consent Agreement Form to be eligible for prizes.
What if I want to change teams?
If you need to make changes to your team, you should discuss them during the team formation process after the team list is shared on October 4. You may also reach out to the organizers if you need further assistance.
If my team doesn’t continue in the Hackathon, how can I still participate?
You can contact Dr. Nara (anara@sdsu.edu) to discuss options for rejoining or forming a new team.
What is the minimum and maximum number of team members required for the Hackathon?
Teams can have a maximum of 4 members. We recommend working in teams, but if you have a specific reason for working alone, reach out to Dr. Nara for special arrangements.
Can my team be eligible for multiple awards?
Yes, teams can be eligible for multiple awards as long as they meet the criteria for each category.
How do we obtain the award money if our team wins an award?
The award distribution process will be explained at the Hackathon. Typically, the prize will be divided among the team members.
Who do I contact if I have more questions about team formation?
For any additional questions, you can reach out to Dr. Nara at anara@sdsu.edu.
